# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Location pastors and leadership at Emmanuel Christian Center (four locations: SLP, ELK, LV, MG). They review plan-versus-actual service timing after the weekend — typically scanning summaries between meetings, not living in the tool. A smaller operator group (comms/production) handles data verification and corrections, but leadership consumption is the primary design target. Internal code and database identifiers retain the historical `campus` and `triage` names.

## Product Purpose

Show how weekend services actually ran against their Planning Center plans: broadcast-window adherence, per-slot and per-element variance, and trends across weeks. Success is a leader understanding the weekend's timing story in under a minute, and trusting the numbers enough to act on them.

## Brand Personality

Executive and polished. Confident, composed, presentation-grade — numbers a leader could put on a screen in a staff meeting without apology. Interpretation over raw data; the interface should tell the timing story, not just display measurements.

## Anti-references

- **Spreadsheet dump**: raw dense tables with no hierarchy or interpretation. Every table needs a headline takeaway; every screen needs an obvious "what matters here."
- Generic admin-template SaaS (KPI card grids, chart junk) — implied by the executive bar: polish means restraint, not dashboard clichés.

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with the verdict** — every view answers "how did the weekend go?" before offering detail.
2. **Interpretation over inventory** — summarize and rank; never present raw rows as the primary reading.
3. **Boardroom-grade polish** — typography, alignment, and charts should hold up projected in a staff meeting.
4. **Evidence on demand** — leadership sees conclusions; review-state caveats and correction evidence stay one click deeper, preserved but not shouted.
5. **Four locations, one standard** — location comparisons are neutral and consistent; the design never reads as a scoreboard shaming a location or worship leader.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG mandate (internal tool, known audience). Keep body text legible against the glass/translucent surfaces and preserve keyboard focus visibility; don't let polish erode baseline readability.
